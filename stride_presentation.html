Context

Multiverse has a scaling constraint

Growing demand for AI upskilling is outpacing Multiverse's service capacity. The path to the next £50M requires a product-led answer.

The Current Constraint

1 coach : 30 learners — the current ratio caps growth without proportional hiring
£79.6M revenue, predominantly services-led — high COGS, limited software leverage
22,000+ active learners but post-programme AI usage drops sharply without ongoing support
1,500 enterprise clients pressure-testing ROI — completion metrics no longer satisfy buyers
Leadership has no visibility — CHROs/CIOs invest £££ in AI tools but can't measure adoption, usage patterns, or business impact
£1.7B valuation — the market sees the potential; the product needs to unlock it

The Market Signal

Enterprise buyers have shifted from "train our people" to "prove it's working" — ROI visibility is the new entry criterion
Competitors (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning) own knowledge delivery but have no behaviour layer — this is the white space
AI adoption failure is now a board-level risk — every CHRO and CIO is accountable for a number
PLG models are now accepted in enterprise — Notion, Figma, Slack all proved the motion works
Multiverse's moat — 1,500 trusted enterprise relationships + coaching methodology is a distribution advantage no startup has

My conviction: I'm betting that Stride transforms Multiverse's coaching IP into a software-first behaviour change engine. One coach can support 100+ learners through strategic touchpoints — not by replacing human connection, but by making the system self-reinforcing between sessions. This is force multiplication, not headcount reduction.

The Product

Five interlocking pillars that replace a one-time training event with a self-reinforcing adoption system. Built for three personas: Learners who adopt, Managers who coach, Admins who measure.

Managers

Admins (CHRO/CIO)

Conservative Forecast — Validation Over Velocity
Philosophy: Q2 EAP is 10 paying enterprise clients at discounted pricing (£40K-£60K vs £78K GA). All EAP clients are existing Multiverse renewals where Stride is bundled. 3-5 clients receive intensive support (weekly check-ins, co-design sessions) in exchange for case study rights. Revenue forecasts assume baseline progression velocity (36%) and conservative expansion rates. Target: 10% penetration of 1,500 Multiverse clients by Year 1. Risk level: Low. Year 1 is about validating outcome-based pricing, not maximizing revenue.
Aggressive Forecast — Velocity Over Validation
Philosophy: 15 EAP clients in Q2, scaling to 250 total accounts by Year 1 (17% penetration). Blended ACV increases to £70K via mix of larger accounts (700+ employees vs 500) and higher progression velocity (40% vs 36%). Requires significantly larger team (45 vs 35), more coaches (175 vs 30), and higher sales capacity (8 AEs vs 5). Trade-off: Higher revenue but adds £1M burn and requires betting big before EAP validation completes. Risk level: Medium. Success depends on strong sales execution and product maturity in Q2-Q3.
